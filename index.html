Our Story

The Art of
Nourishment

KŌEN — meaning "luminous garden" — was born from a decade of study in Kyoto's oldest kaiseki kitchens and a deep love for the unexpected. Chef Hiroshi Tanaka returned to New York with a simple conviction: that Japanese cuisine, at its truest, is not about restriction but about listening.

Listening to the season. To the ingredient. To the guest. Every dish at KŌEN begins with a question: what does this moment want to say?

🌿
Seasonal Sourcing

Ingredients are chosen weekly from trusted farms and fisheries — no frozen shortcuts.

🔥
Ancient Technique

We practice traditional Japanese culinary methods passed down through generations.

✦
Ma Philosophy

The Japanese concept of "negative space" — we believe in what is not on the plate.

🍶
Zero Waste Kitchen

Dashi is made from yesterday's trimmings. Nothing is wasted. Everything is honored.

Hiroshi Tanaka
Executive Chef & Founder
